Inspiring Students via 4 Subteams

Lakota Robotics empowers students to develop skills in coding, CAD, mechanical design, project management, and leadership—skills that prepare them for success in college, careers, and life. Our program fosters collaboration between students, schools, mentors, and local businesses, creating a true community of innovation and opportunity.

Using our custom-built apps for scouting, we collect metrics for scoring using teleop and auton page. We track percentage of times the robot broke, a-stopped, or e-stopped and position of hang using a designated endgame page. And at the end the match we use our final page to collect any penalties, defense, passing, and general assessment about the team scouted.

When a robot is broken, we have a page in our pit scouting app to go ask questions to the team about the issue. We also have a general pit scouting page to collect information about the robot, the team, and any notes that might be helpful for alliance selection. We use this information to help us determine which teams we want to pick as alliance partners and to help us know how to work with them if we do pick them.

With the match scouting and pit scouting, we then create a power bi report to collect the data and turn it into the information we need to pick our alliance partners.

We capture strategic robot concepts from teams at events so we can compare ideas, discuss tradeoffs, and bring the best concepts back to our team for future design decisions.

Lakota Robotics follows the internationally recognized FIRST® Robotics model, offering programs that inspire curiosity, problem-solving, and teamwork.

FLL Tournament since 2006 (20 years)

Paul George, a founding mentor of Lakota Robotics, will always be remembered as a champion for education, innovation, and mentorship. His belief in every student inspired countless young minds to explore STEM and take bold risks.

Alongside Lead Mentor Jimmy Nichols, Paul dreamed of a permanent home for Lakota Robotics—one that would make STEM accessible to all. He often said, “Who are we to decide who deserves to be inspired?” a belief that guides our commitment to keep participation free.

When Paul passed from cancer in 2020, he left the funds to make that dream a reality. The Paul George STEM Center stands as a lasting tribute to his vision, generosity, and the motto behind every robot we build: WWPGD.

Expanding STEM Career Exposure & Resources

Lakota Robotics provides students with hands-on STEM experiences, industry partnerships, and career pathways through workshops, camps, outreach, events, and the Paul George STEM Center, the team strengthens local STEM infrastructure.

Building a Sustainable Program & Leadership Pipeline

The team fosters long-term sustainability through strong culture, leadership development, mentoring younger teams, continuous improvement processes, and partnerships that create a pipeline from student to mentor.

Overcoming Unconscious Bias & Promoting Inclusion

Team 1038 creates equitable access to STEM by reaching under-served, rural, and diverse communities through inclusive outreach programs that ensure every student—regardless of background or experience—has the opportunity to participate.

Removing Financial Barriers to Participation

Team 1038 eliminates cost obstacles by offering free participation, travel grants, and meals, ensuring that high-quality STEM education is accessible to all interested students.
