Lakota Robotics: Inspiring Tomorrow's Innovators. Lakota Robotics is a community-based STEM education program located at the Paul George STEM Center in Liberty Township, Ohio. We proudly represent students from more than 14 school districts across Greater Cincinnati and provide hands-on robotics, engineering, and technology experiences for students in grades 4-12.

Our mission is simple: to inspire young people to explore science and technology through creative, hands-on learning that builds confidence, leadership, and real-world skills. We prepare students to make meaningful contributions in their communities and the world.

Lakota Robotics empowers students to develop skills in coding, CAD, mechanical design, project management, and leadership—skills that prepare them for success in college, careers, and life. Our program fosters collaboration between students, schools, mentors, and local businesses, creating a true community of innovation and opportunity.

Lakota Robotics follows the internationally recognized FIRST® Robotics model, offering programs that inspire curiosity, problem-solving, and teamwork.

FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) - For students in grades 7-12, where teams design, build, and program full-scale robots to compete at regional and national events.

Our impact goes far beyond competition. Lakota Robotics hosts STEM camps, Girl Scout workshops, and community events that inspire students, build teamwork, and spark lifelong interest in STEM.

These programs, along with our annual fundraisers, help sustain the Paul George STEM Center—expanding resources, funding new initiatives, and keeping robotics free for every student.

Community Demo's, Sponsor Demo's, UCBMA Food truck rally and more...

Everyone has a place at Lakota Robotics, from sponsors and mentors to students. Whether you’d like to volunteer, become a corporate partner, or attend one of our fundraising events, your involvement helps us continue to make STEM education accessible to all.

Paul George, a founding mentor of Lakota Robotics, will always be remembered as a champion for education, innovation, and mentorship. His belief in every student inspired countless young minds to explore STEM and take bold risks.

Alongside Lead Mentor Jimmy Nichols, Paul dreamed of a permanent home for Lakota Robotics—one that would make STEM accessible to all. He often said, “Who are we to decide who deserves to be inspired?” a belief that guides our commitment to keep participation free.

When Paul passed from cancer in 2020, he left the funds to make that dream a reality. The Paul George STEM Center stands as a lasting tribute to his vision, generosity, and the motto behind every robot we build: WWPGD.
